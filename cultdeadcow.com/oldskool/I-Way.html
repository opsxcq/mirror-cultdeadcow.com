CULT OF THE DEAD COW


This in many ways is the archetypal zine. Published by Ross Messiah, a
self-described cyberpunk who goes by the handle Swamp Ratte, Cult of the Dead
Cow (cDc) is full of Generation X insights and outlooks, ramblings 
and rages, fiction and humor. This is from number 293:


[insert papagraph from cDc #293]


	Messiah, who lives in Lubbock, Texas, and owns a small recording 
studio, has been publishing cDc since 1985.  "It's the longest published zine 
under the  same publisher," he says. Back when he started the zine, cDc was a 
hacking/phreaking group -- its members were "into phone stuff."

	Messiah, who's 24 and single, works on cDc with about 20 friends.  
About every other month he releases 10 articles or so, posting the ASCII files
 to several Usenet newsgroups and to the Cascade Connections FTP Archives.

	It's the ease of publishing that captivated Messiah.  "It doesn't cost
anything," he says.  "There's no capital involved.  That's the really cool 
thing about it, you just write it and put it out."
