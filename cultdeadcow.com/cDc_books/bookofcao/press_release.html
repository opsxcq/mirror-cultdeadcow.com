CULT OF THE DEAD COW (cDc) Unveils The Book of Cao

Collection of spiritual text files intended to enlighten the unwashed masses.

New York, NY (PRWEB) August 28, 2006 — CULT OF THE DEAD COW (cDc), a prominent group of technology activists and inspiring thought leaders, has just released its first book, entitled The Book of Cao: Enlightenment through a Poke in the Eye.

The Book of Cao is a collection of cDc text files, designed to relate the group's spiritual beliefs. Available for the notable price of $13.37, this book is perfect for daily devotional study and global evangelizing. It can be purchased online at http://www.lulu.com/content/381240. Proceeds from all book sales will be used to sponsor cDc missionaries deployed around the world.

"This book is chock full of life-changing genius," says avid cDc text file reader RaD Man, founder of ACiD Productions (http://www.acid.org). "cDc publishing a book of its spiritual beliefs is the next logical step in its mission to subvert the world's populace through sheer ass-kickery."

While this book marks its first foray into the world of print media, the cDc is no stranger to publishing. For the last two decades, it has released a stream of riveting text files (http://www.cultdeadcow.com/cms/textfile_index.php3), brilliantly addressing various cultural and technology topics. However, the spiritual aspect of the cDc's work has been somewhat overlooked. Until now...

ABOUT CULT OF THE DEAD COW
Founded in Lubbock, TX, CULT OF THE DEAD COW (cDc) is the most influential hacking group in the world. The cDc alumni list reads like a Who's Who of hacking and includes a former Presidential advisor on Internet security, among others. The group is further distinguished by publishing the longest running e-zine on the Internet [est. 1984], stretching the limits of the First Amendment, and fighting anyone or any government that aspires to limit free speech.
